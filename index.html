About

I work where analytics meets accountability — building the data & AI systems that help one of the world's largest companies stay compliant.

At Walmart Global Ethics & Compliance (IROCC), my work spans firearms compliance, food safety, product recalls, environmental health & safety, and seller integrity. The common thread: taking a slow, manual, regulatory-critical process and rebuilding it as an automated, monitored, self-service data product.

I own the full stack of a data product — pipeline → model → application → adoption. That includes LLM applications on Walmart's internal multi-model gateway (among them the first text-to-SQL AI system in the organization), full-stack web applications with Flask and BigQuery backends, and executive dashboards that leadership actually uses to make decisions.

Method matters to me: statistical testing, KPI design, and root-cause analysis first — then the right amount of AI, whether that's text-to-SQL, agentic workflows, RAG, or human-in-the-loop review. I also mentor analysts and drive team adoption of AI tooling and engineering standards.

Product Removal Messaging System

A production recall-messaging platform for Sam's Club that replaced a manual, vendor-dependent process — designed to notify over a million members about product recalls, reliably.

The problem

Recall notifications depended on a third-party vendor with slow turnaround; the business needed self-service messaging with auditability at massive scale.

What I built

A three-layer architecture: a Dataiku web frontend, a Flask backend with 14+ API endpoints for member lookup and branded email preview, and a scenario worker for long-running bulk sends — crash-resumable JSONL streaming at constant memory, 10 parallel SMTP workers, automatic retry passes, and BigQuery audit logging with 7-year retention.

Outcome

~$100K in annual vendor savings and notification turnaround cut from days to hours. Business feedback rated it more intuitive than the existing enterprise tool, and leadership positioned it to become Walmart's central recall-messaging platform. A companion Power BI recall lookup tool extended search across private-brand, General Merchandise, and OTC recalls.

AI Classification Review Application

A full-stack review workbench where AI does the first pass on product-safety classification and humans make the final call — built to dissolve a 60,000-items-per-quarter manual review bottleneck.

The problem

Product-safety item classification was reviewed entirely by hand — tens of thousands of items per quarter, each needing an action/no-action decision.

What I built

An application with 8 Flask API endpoints, 1,100+ lines of Python and 1,400+ lines of JavaScript — batch submission, inline edits, filtering, tagging, and export. The BigQuery layer uses server-side pagination, window-function deduplication, and single-statement bulk updates that collapse thousands of row updates into one round trip per batch.

Outcome

Phase I projects 1,040 review hours saved in 2026, with formal stakeholder review sessions shaping a Phase II roadmap for the full review pipeline.

LLM Seller-Compliance Pipelines

Three production pipelines built in partnership with the Data Science team, applying LLM classification to marketplace seller integrity at scale.

The problem

Seller complaints and takedown obligations (Take It Down Act, DMCA) arrived as unstructured incident records — over 120,000 of them — that had to be classified, scored, and connected to orders, sellers, and items.

What I built

A Take It Down Act classifier combining a 100-term curated dictionary and deterministic signal-tier scoring with LLM classification under a structured output schema; a DMCA relevance-scoring pipeline; and a complaint-enrichment pipeline with dual-path entity resolution (order number + reporter contact). Delta-load BigQuery processing, 10-worker multithreading, retry logic, and business-confirmed false-positive suppression throughout.

Outcome

120K+ incident records resolved to order, seller, partner, customer, and item level — giving the compliance team a triaged, prioritized queue instead of a raw feed.

Food Safety Certification Automation

End-to-end automation of a fully manual certification-tracking process — from third-party exam provider to store-level completion dashboards.

The problem

Tracking which associates held required food-safety certifications meant manually pulling exam data, matching associates, uploading to the learning system, and comparing against job-code requirements — for every business unit, region, market, store, and club.

What I built

An API-integrated Dataiku pipeline connecting the third-party exam provider to internal learning systems, mapping certifications to job-code-defined requirements and tracking completion percentage at every level. Layered on top: congratulatory and 1x/2x/3x-fail escalation alerting, a retake dashboard analyzing repeat-attempt patterns, and job-code tracking for associates changing roles into certification-required positions.

Outcome

A manual workflow became an always-current automated program across 4,700+ locations — recognized with the org-wide "Data Rockstar" award — with the master data shared onward to workforce-intelligence reporting.

Sanctions Compliance Analytics Pipeline

An enterprise reporting pipeline and dashboard ecosystem helping the Global Sanctions Compliance team monitor items connected to prohibited and sanctioned countries — across first-party and marketplace catalogs in multiple markets.

The problem

OFAC-related sanctions monitoring spanned disconnected item sources across multiple markets, with manual monthly report handling and no consolidated detection process for newly appearing sanctioned-country items.

What I built

Consolidated multi-market 1P and 3P item data into recurring BigQuery reporting tables anchored by a master sanctioned-items dataset, with a Dataiku detection flow that compares weekly item pulls against the master table — automatically notifying the Global Sanctions Compliance team of newly detected items and appending them to the master dataset. Developed the dashboard query logic (item, UPC, vendor, buyer, country-of-origin, prohibited-country indicators) plus stakeholder-requested enhancements like prohibited-country common-name mapping, supplier partner typing, and simple/detailed export views.

Outcome

A recurring detection → notification → master-update monitoring workflow trusted for compliance decisioning — backed by audit-ready documentation of query logic, data connectors, source tables, filters, and access groups that supported Global Audit's review. Also sustained daily vendor non-compliance notification flows and helped define requirements for fully automated data-feed ingestion.
